Books Available

  • Alibaba



    Jack Ma, the founder of the Alibaba group, was never a techie. He was bad at math, failed twice in high school, and got his first computer only at 33. Ma’s route to becoming the billionaire founder of one of the world’s largest technology companies came through English. In the book Ali Baba: The House That Jack Ma Built, author Duncan Clark, a former Morgan Stanley investment banker and fluent Mandarin speaker, traces Ma’s story from his early days, through his first Internet venture, Chinapages.com, and then Alibaba. He has had a ringside view of much of this, having lived and worked in Shanghai, and even consulted for Alibaba.
